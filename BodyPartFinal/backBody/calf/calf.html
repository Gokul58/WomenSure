Calf


The calf is the back portion of the lower leg in human anatomy. The muscles within the calf correspond to the posterior compartment of the leg. The two largest muscles within this compartment are known together as the calf muscle and attach to the heel via the Achilles tendon. Several other, smaller muscles attach to the knee, the ankle, and via long tendons to the toes.

Featured Exercise

Leg Lift Exercise

  1. Press lower back into the floor. While lowering legs, stop once you feel your back lifting off the floor.
  2. Keep legs together and inner thighs engaged.
  3. Inhale on the way down, exhale on the way up.

Featured Yoga

Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana)

  1. Bring both legs outstretched for a forward bend.
  2. Lengthen the spine in a seated position before coming forward. Imagine the pelvis as a bowl that is tipping forward as you come down.

Home Remedy

Massage With Essential Oils

  1. Put a small amount of warm lotion or oil on your hands. Rub your hands together so the lotion is spread evenly on your hands.
  2. Move your hands slowly during the massage to help the person relax.
  3. Start massaging the back at the lower back. Move your hands upward on both sides of the spine all the way to the shoulders.
  4. Make a circular motion as you move your hands upward. Press a bit more firmly with your thumbs as you make the circles.
  5. Move across the shoulders and start moving down the upper arms. Use less pressure as you move downward.
  6. Ask if you are applying too much or too little pressure as you massage. Ask the person to tell you if he feels pain in any area. Do not massage a painful area, or massage it very gently.
  7. Apply more lotion on your hands as needed.
